Jannik Sinner, currently ranked 1st in the world, cruised past Alexander Zverev, currently ranked 2nd, to win his third grand slam title. The championship was far from controversy, though, as both players face heckling crowds, doping allegations, potential suspensions, and domestic abuse accusations.

Just months before the US Open, in March 2024, Sinner failed two drug tests that showed traces of clostebol in his system. Players, fans, and tennis officials have expressed many polarizing opinions about Sinner's case, as some called for an immediate suspension while others defended the young star, pointing to potential contamination from authorized medications.

The Italian Tennis Federation launched an investigation, working closely with the International Tennis Federation (ITF) and World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) to examine the case. Sinner maintained his innocence throughout the process, suggesting the positive results may have come from a prescribed medication he had been taking for a shoulder injury.

Despite the controversy, Sinner was permitted to continue playing while the investigation proceeded, leading to heated debates within the tennis community. Several top players spoke out about the situation, including former world number one Novak Djokovic. "This circumstance is not positive for our sport, so hopefully we can resolve this case as soon as possible," Djokovic said in a press conference after the Shanghai Open.
